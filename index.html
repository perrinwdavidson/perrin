Hey, I'm Perrin.

I'm an undergraduate at the University of Chicago where I am working toward a B.A. in Physics with Honors and a B.S. in Computational and Applied Mathematics with Honors.

Research Interests

Currently, I work under Professor David Archer in the Department of Geophysical Sciences at UChicago and under Dr. Ken O. Buesseler at the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution. In both labs, I work to develop data-constrained oceanographic models using statistical and physical techniques. Specifically, I am interested in applying machine learning to inverse modeling and regression problems to further reduce error in theoretical carbon model output.

In my work, I commonly come back to the statistics aphorism attributed to George E. P. Box

"All models are wrong, but some are useful."
It is therefore my job as an aspiring modeler to make all my models useful. I mean, who wants a worthless model? I certainly don't...
